### Public Key Certificate
***

A **Public Key Certificate** also known as a digital certificate is a cryptographic document operated by computers to verify the identity of a named party when communicating over the internet or other network

These certificates typically consist of the following:

1. Digital Signature of a [[Certificate Authority]]
2. Sender Public Key
3. Additional Identifying Information

The public key certificate uses mathematical formulas to handle a variety of functions:

- attach a digital signature to software, documents, and emails
- assert the certificate holder’s identity
- encrypt and decrypt data in transit
- secure data transfers and email communications

This is done through asymmetric encryption
