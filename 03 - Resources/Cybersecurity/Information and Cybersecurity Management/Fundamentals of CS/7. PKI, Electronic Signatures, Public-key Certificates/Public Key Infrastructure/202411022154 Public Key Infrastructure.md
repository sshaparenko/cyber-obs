### Public Key Infrastructure
***

**Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)** covers everything that establishes and manages public key encryption.

PKI allows a trusted party to issue, maintain and revoke public key certificates. It enables users to interact with other users and applications to obtain and verify identities and keys from trusted sources.

The main purposes of PKI are the following:
- publish public keys/certificates;
- certify that a key is tied to an individual or entity;
- verify the validity of a public key;
- provide services such as confidentiality, integrity, authenticity, non-repudiation and access control

The PKI primary elements are:
- [[Digital Certificates]]
- [[Certificate Authority]]
- [[Registration Authority]]

#### Using Public Key Infrastructure in Digital World
***
PKI enables secure communication and transactions between governments, businesses and citizens:

- citizens can use PKI to sign documents for tax filings and other government services
- digital identity cards and electronic passports are secured with PKI
- in B2B transactions, S/MIME certificates ensure email authenticity and confidentiality
- digital signatures are used in G2B transactions to verify compliance with regulations and secure procurement processes
- in healthcare PKI-based encryption and digital signatures are used to ensure the authenticity and confidentiality of patient records in electronic health records
- in finance PKI is issued to secure transactions and communications between financial institutions
- in scientific institutions PKI ensures data exchange between researchers and protects scientific results from unauthorized access
- in government PKI-based authentication and encryption secure access to online government services
- in smart offices, PKI ensures secure communication between connected devices such as access control systems and smart lightning
