#### Terminology
***
- **Cryptology** - is a science involved with data transmission and storage in a safe and confidential form. It encloses both cryptography and cryptanalysis
- **Cryptography** - is a method of processing information and communications using codes so that only those from whom the information is intended can read and process it
- **Cryptanalysis** - is the decryption and analysis of codes, ciphers or encrypted text. Cryptanalysis uses mathematical formulas to search for algorithm vulnerabilities and break into cryptography or information security systems

#### Types of Encryption
***

**Symmetric Encryption** uses <u>one single key</u> to encrypt and decrypt data.

Symmetric Encryption algorithms:

- **Data Encryption Standard (DES)** is one of the oldest symmetric encryption methods. Introduced in 1976, DES uses a 56-bit encryption key, and it is based on the Fiestel Structure
- **Advanced Encryption Standard (AES)** is one of the most prevalently used encryption algorithms developed as an alternative to the DES algorithm. AES works on the approaches of substitution and permutation
- **Blowfish** is one of the first safe block ciphers and is freely available for anyone. It’s fast and provides a reasonable encryption rate
- **Twofish** is the successor of Blowfish, considered the fastest encryption algorithm and is perfect for both hardware and software environments. It includes advanced functionalities to replace the DES algorithm
- **Rivest Cipher (RC4)** is a stream cipher created in 1987 that operates on data a byte at a time to encrypt that data.

**Asymmetric Encryption** uses <u>a pair of public and private keys</u> for encryption

Asymmetric Encryption algorithms:

- **ECDSA (Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm)** is one of the most complex asymmetric cryptography encryption algorithms and is mainly used to create pseudo-random numbers, digital signatures and more.
- **Diffie-Hellman** is used to estabish a shared secret that can be used for secret communications while exchanging data over a public network using the elliptic curve to generate points and get the secret key using the parameters
- **RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman)** todat is the most widely used asymmetric algorithms for specific security ervices or purposes. It enables public key encryption t osecure sensitive data, practically when sent over an insecure network such as the Internet
- **Digital Signature Algorithm (DSA)** can be used only for signing data and it cannot be used for encryption
- **EIGamal Encryption Algorithm** uses asymmetric key encryption to communicate between two parties and encrypt the message
